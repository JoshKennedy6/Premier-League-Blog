Overview

The Premier League is the top level of professional soccer in England and is widely considered one of the most exciting and competitive leagues in the world. It was formed in 1992 when leading clubs broke away from the old Football League to create a new, more commercially driven competition. Today, 20 teams compete each season from August to May, playing 38 matches each, and the club with the most points at the end wins the title while the bottom three are relegated to the EFL Championship. What makes it special is its intensity and unpredictability — even smaller clubs can be traditional giants like Manchester United, Liverpool F.C., Arsenal F.C., and Manchester City F.C. — which is a big reason why it has such a massive global following.
